Long-term Management
  • Remove Affected Leaves
    Carefully prune and dispose of all infected leaves. Do not compost them as the fungus can survive.
  • Adjust Watering Practices
    Water at the base of plants early in the day to allow leaves to dry quickly. Avoid overhead watering.
  • Improve Air Circulation
    Space plants properly and prune excess foliage to improve airflow around plants.
  • Apply Mulch
    Add a layer of organic mulch around plants to prevent soil splashing onto leaves during rain or watering.
  • Baking Soda Spray
    Mix 1 tablespoon baking soda, 2.5 tablespoons vegetable oil, and 1 gallon water. Spray every 7-10 days.
  • Neem Oil Application
    Apply neem oil solution (follow package instructions) every 7-14 days as both treatment and prevention.
  • Copper Fungicide
    Use organic copper-based fungicide according to label instructions, especially during humid conditions.
  • Systemic Fungicides
    Use products containing chlorothalonil or mancozeb according to label instructions for severe infections.
  • Safety Precautions
    Always wear protective equipment and follow all safety guidelines when using chemical treatments.
  • Crop Rotation
    Avoid planting the same crop family in the same location for at least 2-3 years to break disease cycles.
  • Resistant Varieties
    Choose disease-resistant plant varieties for future plantings to reduce susceptibility.
  • Soil Health Management
    Maintain balanced soil fertility with proper pH and organic matter to support plant health.

Prevention Strategies

  • Crop Rotation
    Avoid planting tomatoes in the same location for at least 2-3 years.
  • Resistant Varieties
    Choose disease-resistant tomato varieties for future plantings.
  • Regular Monitoring
    Inspect plants weekly for early signs of disease and treat immediately.
  • Proper Nutrition
    Maintain balanced soil fertility to keep plants healthy and more resistant to diseases.
